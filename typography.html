How to NOT burn popcorn

To avoid burning popcorn, you can: Use a heavy-bottomed pot Cheap pots don't distribute heat evenly, which can lead to hot spots that burn the popcorn. Adjust the heat After the first pops, turn the heat down to medium or medium-low so the kernels have time to pop without burning. Shake the pot Shake the pot every minute or so to ensure all the kernels get a chance to pop. Use a dry pan You can pop popcorn in a dry pan or skillet without oil. Test the temperature Start with two kernels to test the temperature. Once they pop, the oil is hot enough.

Add a lid slightly While the popcorn is popping, you can slightly tip the lid to allow steam to escape and prevent the popcorn from steaming itself in the pot. Season immediately Season the popcorn right away so the heat helps the seasoning stick. Use clarified butter Regular butter has a lower smoke point than the temperature needed for popcorn to pop, so it can burn before the popcorn is done. If you're microwaving popcorn, you can use a separate plate to reduce heat stress on the microwave's glass plate.
